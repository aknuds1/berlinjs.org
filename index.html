Deploying a Node.js App on Google Container Engine

Arve Knudsen

Google Container Engine (GKE) is Google's service for hosting Kubernetes managed applications. Kubernetes itself being Google's open source container (Docker/Rkt) management system, enabling you to create scalable clusters of app containers.

In the MuzHack project, which uses Node.js on the server, we have chosen to rely on Docker and, furthermore, deploy to Google Container Engine with its Kubernetes management engine. Our experience so far suggests that Kubernetes is the way forward for deploying Web services, bearing in mind it is still a maturing technology.
